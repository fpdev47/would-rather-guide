Speaking with WOULD 💬

Would  ·  Would Like  ·  Would Rather  ·  Prefer  ·  Second Conditional

Your English Buddy  |  Basic–Intermediate Level

Lesson Goals

Section 1
  • Talk about preferences — what you like, want, or choose
  • Speak politely when asking for things
  • Compare two choices and say which one you prefer
  • Talk about imaginary situations ("what if…")
  • Answer personal questions in real English
  • Use would, would like, would rather, prefer, and the second conditional

Quick Meaning Guide

Section 2

Before we start — here's what each expression means. We'll study each one in detail soon.

ExpressionMeaningQuick Example
I would…Something I'd do (hypothetical)I would help.
I would like…A polite want or wishI would like to rest.
I would rather…I prefer this over thatI'd rather stay home.
I wouldn't…I would NOT do itI wouldn't go alone.

Contractions — Short Forms

Section 3

In speaking and informal writing, we use short forms. They sound natural and friendly.

Full FormShort FormExample

Core Rule: What Comes After WOULD?

Section 4

This rule applies to every use of would in this lesson:

WOULD + BASE VERB

The base verb = the simple dictionary form. No changes.

✅ Correct

I would go.

She would help.

They would work.

❌ Wrong

I would to go.

She would helping.

They would worked.

💡 Think of it this way

"Would" already does the work. The next verb just sits there — simple and unchanged.

🚫 Never add

to → I would to go ❌

-ing → I would going ❌

-ed → I would worked ❌

🗣️

WOULD

Section 5
🎙️ Speaking Warm-Up — Answer Out Loud!
  1. Would you like to be rich? Why?
  2. Would you like to have a better job? What kind?
  3. Would you like to speak perfect English? Why?
  4. Would you like to live in a different city?
  5. Would you like to have more free time?

What Does WOULD Mean?

We use would to talk about things we imagine, wish for, or want to do — but they are not happening right now. It also makes questions and offers sound polite.

Structure

Subject + WOULD / WOULDN'T + BASE VERB
TypeExample
Affirmative (+)I would love to travel more.
Negative (−)I wouldn't eat that. It looks bad.
Question (?)Would you like some help?
Short answerYes, I would.  /  No, I wouldn't.

Everyday Examples

  • I would love a day off.
  • She would like a cold drink.
  • We would prefer an earlier shift.
  • He wouldn't eat spicy food.
  • They would enjoy a longer break.

Work Examples

  • I would ask my supervisor, but he's not here.
  • She would help with the boxes — she's very strong.
  • We wouldn't lift that alone. It's too heavy.
  • Would you carry this to aisle 3, please?

Polite Questions & Offers

Would is very useful for polite requests:

  • Would you like some water?
  • Would you help me with this, please?
  • Would you like to take a break?
  • I would like to speak with a nurse.
💡 Key Point

WOULD describes something imagined, wished for, or offered — not a real action happening right now.

✅ Good Answers

"Yes, I would. I'd love to be rich!"

"No, I wouldn't. I prefer my life here."

Always answer with would or wouldn't.

⚠️ Watch Out

After WOULD, the verb does not change.

✅ I would love
❌ I would loves
❌ I would loved

WOULD vs. WOULD LIKE

Section 6
🎙️ Speaking Warm-Up — Answer Out Loud!
  1. What would you like to eat after class today?
  2. Would you like to have an easier work schedule?
  3. What would you like to learn in English this year?
  4. Would you like to sit down and rest right now?
  5. Would you like to see a doctor if you had stomach pain?

The Two Patterns Side by Side

WOULD + BASE VERB
WOULD LIKE + TO + BASE VERB
WOULD + base verbWOULD LIKE + to + base verb
I would study more.I would like to study more.
I would go home early.I would like to go home early.
She would help us.She would like to help us.
They would leave now.They would like to leave now.

Both are correct — WOULD LIKE sounds softer and more polite.

Correct vs. Incorrect

✅ Correct

I would study. (no TO)

I would like to study. (TO needed)

Would you like to sit?

❌ Wrong

I would to study.

I would like study. (missing TO)

Would you like sitting?

Real-Life Examples

  • I would like to take a break. I'm very tired.
  • She would like to move to a day shift.
  • Would you like some water? You look thirsty.
  • I would like to speak to the nurse. I have a stomachache.
💡 The Big Difference

WOULD + base verb
→ I would help.

WOULD LIKE + TO + base verb
→ I would like to help.

🚫 Common Error

Students forget the TO after WOULD LIKE.

❌ I would like eat.
✅ I would like to eat.

☕ Polite Tip

"Would you like…?" is much more polite than "Do you want…?"

Use it to offer things to people!

🔀

WOULD RATHER

Section 7
🎙️ Speaking Warm-Up — Answer Out Loud!
  1. Would you rather work in the morning or at night?
  2. Would you rather drink coffee or tea right now?
  3. Would you rather stay home or go out after work today?
  4. Would you rather rest or go for a walk if you felt tired?
  5. Would you rather have a short break or a long lunch at work?

Pattern 1 — Simple Choice

Subject + WOULD RATHER + BASE VERB
  • I would rather stay home.
  • She would rather drink coffee.
  • He would rather work the day shift.

Pattern 2 — Comparing with THAN

WOULD RATHER + BASE VERB + than + BASE VERB
  • I'd rather speak English than Spanish.
  • She'd rather drink coffee than tea.
  • They'd rather stay home than go out.

Pattern 3 — Questions with OR

WOULD you + RATHER + BASE VERB or BASE VERB ?
  • Would you rather go to the beach or stay home?
  • Would you rather work today or rest?

Correct vs. Incorrect

✅ Correct

I'd rather go home.

I'd rather walk than drive.

Would you rather walk or drive?

❌ Wrong

I'd rather to go home.

I'd rather going home.

Would you rather to walk?

⚠️ THAN vs. OR

THAN = statements
"I'd rather A than B."

OR = questions
"Would you rather A or B?"

🚫 No TO, No -ING

After WOULD RATHER, always use the base verb.

❌ I'd rather to eat.
❌ I'd rather eating.
✅ I'd rather eat.

💡 Meaning

WOULD RATHER = I choose this over that right now, in this moment.

❤️

PREFER

Section 8
🎙️ Speaking Warm-Up — Answer Out Loud!
  1. Do you prefer rice or pasta for lunch?
  2. Do you prefer day shifts or night shifts?
  3. Do you prefer to study at home or in a class?
  4. Do you prefer hot weather or cold weather?
  5. Do you prefer tea or water when you have a stomach ache?

Key Patterns — Learn These!

Pattern 1 — Noun

Subject + PREFER + NOUN

I prefer coffee.  /  She prefers the day shift.

Pattern 2 — Noun to Noun (comparison)

PREFER + NOUN + to + NOUN

I prefer coffee to tea.  /  She prefers water to soda.

Pattern 3 — -ing verb

PREFER + VERB + -ing

I prefer working mornings.  /  He prefers eating light meals.

Pattern 4 — to + base verb

PREFER + to + BASE VERB

I prefer to work mornings.  /  She prefers to eat early.

PREFER vs. WOULD RATHER

PREFER — general habitWOULD RATHER — this moment
I prefer coffee. (always)I'd rather drink tea right now.
She prefers mornings. (in general)She'd rather work tonight. (today)
We prefer light meals.We'd rather eat something quick today.
⚠️ Common Error #1

When comparing two nouns, use to — NOT "than"!

✅ I prefer coffee to tea.
❌ I prefer coffee than tea.

⚠️ Common Error #2

When using a verb after prefer, you can use -ing or to + base — both are correct!

✅ I prefer working mornings.
✅ I prefer to work mornings.
❌ I prefer work mornings.

💡 PREFER vs. RATHER

Prefer = in general, always

Would rather = right now, today

Second Conditional — "What If…"

Section 9
🎙️ Speaking Warm-Up — Answer Out Loud!
  1. What would you do if you had one completely free day?
  2. What would you do if you felt very sick at work?
  3. If you had more money, what would you buy first?
  4. If you spoke English very well, what kind of job would you like?
  5. If you had a bad stomachache at work, what would you do first?

The Formula

IF + PAST SIMPLE , WOULD + BASE VERB
IF clause (imaginary)WOULD clause (result)
If I had more time,I would study more.
If she lived closer,she would arrive earlier.
If I felt sick,I would go home.
If we had a bigger break,we would eat a real lunch.

You can also flip the order — same meaning:
I would study more if I had more time.

Work & Health Examples

  • If I had a different schedule, I would sleep much better.
  • If my coworker had too many boxes, I would help them.
  • If I had a stomachache, I would tell my supervisor.
  • If I felt dizzy, I would sit down and ask for help.
  • If someone needed help, I would call 911 immediately.

English & Life Examples

  • If I spoke English very well, I would apply for a better job.
  • If I had more confidence, I would speak more in class.
  • If I had more money, I would travel to another country.
💡 Why Past Tense?

The past verb in the IF clause does NOT mean past time.

It shows the situation is not real right now — it's imaginary.

"If I had money…" = I don't have it now. Just imagining!

🚫 Never use WILL

❌ If I had money, I will travel.
✅ If I had money, I would travel.

⚠️ Watch Out

❌ If I would have time, I go.
✅ If I had time, I would go.

Speaking Practice

Section 12

Answer each question out loud. Use full sentences and add one extra detail.

A — Questions with WOULD

  1. What would you do if you finished work two hours early today?
  2. Would you take a different job if someone offered you one? Why?
  3. What would you eat for dinner if you could choose anything?
  4. Would you call a doctor if you had chest pain at work?
  5. Would you ask your coworker for help if you had too many boxes?
  6. What would you do if you had no phone for one full day?

B — Would You Rather Questions

  1. Would you rather work the morning shift or the night shift?
  2. Would you rather eat a big lunch or a small snack at work?
  3. Would you rather take the bus or drive to work?
  4. Would you rather have Friday off or Monday off?
  5. Would you rather rest at home or exercise after work?
  6. Would you rather drink water or a sports drink when very tired?
  7. Would you rather talk to a doctor in English or in Spanish?
  8. Would you rather have more money or more free time?

C — PREFER Questions

  1. Do you prefer to work alone or with a team?
  2. Do you prefer rice or bread with your meals?
  3. Do you prefer studying in the morning or at night?
  4. Do you prefer hot drinks or cold drinks when you're sick?
  5. Do you prefer a short break during the shift or a long one at the end?
  6. Do you prefer to communicate by phone call or by text?

D — Second Conditional

  1. If you had three days off, where would you go?
  2. If you spoke perfect English, what would change in your life?
  3. If you had a bad headache at work, what would you do?
  4. If you could change one thing about your work schedule, what would it be?
  5. If a coworker had a medical emergency, what would you do?
  6. If you had more money, what would be the first thing you'd buy?
  7. If you had more confidence in English, what would you do differently?

How to Answer Better

Section 14
StrategySimple AnswerBetter Answer
Answer in full sentences"Coffee.""I would like to drink coffee."
Add a reason with because"I'd rather rest.""I'd rather rest because I'm very tired."
Add one extra detail"I would go home.""I would go home and sleep for a few hours."
Compare two ideas"I prefer mornings.""I prefer mornings to nights because I sleep better."
💡 Remember

Even one extra word makes your answer sound more natural!

"Yes." → "Yes, I would." → "Yes, I would, because I like helping people."

Homework

Section 15

A. Writing Task

Write 8–12 sentences about yourself. Use would, would rather, prefer, and the second conditional.

📝 Your Topic

Write about what you would like to improve in your life, what you would rather change in your daily routine, what you prefer at work or in your study habits, and what you would do if you had more time, money, or confidence in English. Try to use each expression at least once.

B. Written Questions

  1. What would you do if you had a free week with no work?
  2. Would you like to change anything about your current job? What?
  3. Do you prefer to study alone or with someone? Why?
  4. Would you rather live in a big city or a small town? Why?
  5. What would you do if you felt sick at work and couldn't leave?
  6. Do you prefer hot food or cold food? Give an example.
  7. If you had more free time, what would you learn or do?
  8. What would you like to improve about your English? Why?
  9. Would you rather have a short break every hour or one long break? Why?
  10. If you could speak to a doctor in English, what would you say first?
